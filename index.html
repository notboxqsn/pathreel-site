Documentation

Getting Started

Permissions

PathReel requires two permissions to work properly:

  • Location (Always Allow) — Required for background GPS recording. PathReel uses high-accuracy GPS to track your route even when the screen is locked or you switch to other apps. Go to Settings > PathReel > Location > Always.
  • Photo Library (Full Access) — Required to match your photos to routes and save exported videos. PathReel scans photos taken during a trip's time range and places them on the route.

Recording a Trip

On the Record tab, tap "Start Recording". A transport mode picker appears — choose from car, motorcycle, bike, walk, run, boat, or train. PathReel begins tracking your route immediately using high-accuracy GPS (updated every 3 meters of movement).

  • You can lock your phone, switch apps, or put it in your pocket — recording continues in the background.
  • The blue status bar indicator shows that location is being tracked.
  • The map follows your direction of travel with the forward direction always pointing up, like a navigation app.
  • Distance, duration, and point count update in real time on screen.
  • The transport icon changes automatically — if driving speed drops below 8 km/h, a walking icon appears; it switches back to the vehicle icon when speed picks up.
  • Take photos along the way — they will automatically appear on your route during replay.
  • To stop, tap "Stop Recording" and confirm.
  • When a trip ends, PathReel automatically checks if your route passed within 200m of any saved check-in targets and marks them as visited.

Viewing Trip Details

Go to the Trips tab to see all recorded trips. Tap a trip to view its details:

  • Full route displayed on a satellite or standard map.
  • Timeline showing start, end, photos taken along the way, and detected stops.
  • Tap any photo in the timeline to view it full screen.
  • Long-press any timeline item to add a personal note. Notes appear during replay and in exported videos.
  • Long-press the trip card in the list to edit the trip name, description, or delete it (requires biometric authentication).

Replaying a Route

From the trip detail page, tap "Replay Route". The cinematic 3D flyover begins:

  • The camera follows the route in real time with pitch and heading matching the direction of travel.
  • Photos zoom in automatically when the route passes nearby, showing the photo, address, and POI name.
  • Stops are highlighted with a card showing place name, address, and duration.
  • A compass in the top-right corner shows orientation. Real-time clock, distance, and speed show in the top-left panel.
  • Speed control — Tap the speed button (1x/2x/3x) to change playback speed. All route points are rendered at every speed; only the interval between frames changes.
  • Camera height — Tap the altitude button to adjust camera height (0.5x to 3x). Higher shows more area, lower gives more detail.
  • Map style — Toggle between satellite and standard map view.
  • After the route finishes, a 5-second overview zooms out to show the complete route.

Exporting Video

During replay, tap the export button. A bottom sheet appears with aspect ratio options:

  • Original — Native aspect ratio, no cropping.
  • 9:16 — TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, Xiaohongshu (vertical).
  • 3:4 — Xiaohongshu (alternative portrait).
  • 1:1 — Instagram feed, WeChat Moments (square).
  • 4:5 — Instagram feed (portrait).
  • 16:9 — YouTube, X/Twitter (landscape).

After selecting a ratio, PathReel replays the route and captures every frame. The map view resizes to match your chosen ratio so no content is lost. Progress is shown on screen.

Once saved, you can choose to share:

  • Copy & Share — An auto-generated caption is copied to your clipboard (includes city name, weather, temperature, places visited, season, and time of day), then the system share sheet opens for the video.
  • Share Video Only — Opens the share sheet directly without the caption.

The share caption is generated based on the actual trip data — start/end city (via reverse geocoding), weather and temperature at the time of the trip (via Open-Meteo API), notable stops, and more. A different template is randomly selected each time.

Trip Groups

Organize multiple trips into groups — perfect for multi-day vacations or themed collections.

  • Go to Trips tab > tap "Trip Groups" at the top.
  • Tap "+ New Group" to create a group. Enter a name (required) and optional note.
  • Tap a group to open it. Use "Add Trips" to select trips to include (multi-select with checkboxes).
  • A trip can belong to multiple groups. Deleting a group does not delete the trips.
  • Long-press a trip inside a group to remove it. Long-press the group card to edit or delete the group.
  • Group Playback Duration — Each group has its own duration setting (default: 60 seconds total). Tap the duration display to change it (1–300 seconds).
  • Tap "Replay Route" to play all trips in sequence. Between trips, the camera zooms out to show both endpoints, then zooms into the next trip's starting point for a smooth transition.
  • All photos and stops from every trip in the group are included during playback.

Insights

The Insights tab shows your travel statistics:

  • Total distance, driving time, average speed.
  • Records: longest trip, top speed.
  • Heatmap showing everywhere you've been — tap to explore.
  • Filter by 7 days, 30 days, or all time.

Attraction Discovery

The Attractions tab lets you search for nearby points of interest from multiple data sources:

  • Data sources — UNESCO World Heritage Sites, Wikivoyage curated travel guides, US National Park Service (NPS), and Geoapify local points of interest. Results are deduplicated and merged automatically.
  • Search center — Three ways to set where to search: use your current GPS location, long-press the map to drop a pin, or tap the search icon to enter a city/address.
  • Search radius — Drag the slider to set the search radius from 1 km to 50 km.
  • Read More — Tap any attraction to view details. PathReel first tries to find a Wikipedia article in your language (via Wikidata language links). If none exists, it uses AI (Groq Llama 3.3 70B) to generate a description in your language. Images are fetched from Wikidata or Wikipedia geosearch.
  • Text-to-Speech — Tap "Listen" to have the description read aloud. PathReel automatically selects the best available voice (Siri > Enhanced > Default) and detects whether the text is in English or your language to pick the correct voice.
  • Add to Check-in — Tap the ⭐ button on any search result to save it as a check-in target.
  • Custom Places — Add your own places (cafés, friends' houses, memorials) with a name, address, note, and category.

Check-in Stamps

Switch to "My Check-ins" to manage your travel passport:

  • Stats cards — Four tier categories with progress: UNESCO (World Heritage), Featured (Wikivoyage), Local (Geoapify), and My Places (custom). Tap a card to filter the list and map.
  • Map view — All check-in targets displayed on a satellite map. Visited targets show in bright tier colors with ✅; unvisited targets show in gray with the tier icon. Tap a visited marker to view details.
  • Manual check-in — Tap the ✓ button next to an unvisited target. PathReel verifies your GPS position — you must be within 500 meters. The exact distance is recorded.
  • Auto check-in — When you stop a trip recording, PathReel scans all GPS track points and automatically marks any planned target within 200 meters as visited. Distance, GPS coordinates, and trip ID are saved.
  • Check-in detail — Tap a visited record to see: a mini map with both the attraction pin and your check-in location, distance, method (Auto GPS / Manual), timestamp, tier, description, notes, and any photos taken nearby (within 1 km, ±1 hour of check-in time).
  • Delete targets — Long-press (2 seconds) an unvisited target to delete it. Visited records are locked and cannot be deleted or modified.

Settings

Customize your PathReel experience on the Settings tab:

Appearance

  • Appearance — Switch between Day (light) and Night (dark) mode. All screens, modals, maps, and exported videos follow the selected theme.
  • Language — Choose from 16 languages: English, 简体中文, 繁體中文, Español, Français, العربية, Português, Русский, 日本語, 한국어, Deutsch, हिन्दी, Italiano, Türkçe, ไทย, Tiếng Việt. Automatically detects your device language on first launch. All UI elements, attraction descriptions, TTS voice, and share captions adapt to your chosen language.

Replay Settings

  • Photo Display (seconds) — How long a photo stays on screen during route replay and video export. When the route passes a GPS-matched photo, it zooms in and shows the image for this duration. Range: 0.5–10s. Default: 1.5s.
  • Stop Display (seconds) — How long a detected stop card stays on screen during replay and video export. The card shows the place name (via reverse geocoding), address, and stop duration. Range: 0.5–10s. Default: 2s.
  • Stop Min Time (minutes) — The minimum duration you must remain at a location for it to be detected as a "stop." Shorter stays (e.g., traffic lights, quick bathroom breaks) are filtered out. Range: 1–60 min. Default: 10 min.
  • Trip Duration (seconds) — Total playback duration for a single trip replay. PathReel distributes all GPS points evenly across this duration. Shorter = faster flyover, longer = more detail. Range: 5–300s. Default: 30s. Note: Group playback uses the group's own duration setting.

Auto Record (iOS)

Automatically start recording when you leave home and stop when you return — no more forgetting to press the button.

  • Enable Auto Record — Master toggle. When enabled, PathReel monitors a circular geofence around your home location using iOS region monitoring (CLLocationManager). This works even when the app is in the background or terminated.
  • Home Location — Tap to enter your home address. PathReel geocodes the address and shows it on a map with the geofence circle for confirmation. You can retry if the location is wrong.
  • Geofence Radius — The distance from your home location that triggers auto-start/stop. When you exit this circle, recording starts; when you re-enter, recording stops. Range: 100–500m. Default: 200m. Tip: If you live near a busy road, increase the radius to avoid false triggers.
  • Default Mode — The transport mode used when auto-recording starts (car, motorcycle, bike, walk, run, boat, or train). Default: car.
  • Min Stay to Stop — How long you must remain inside the geofence before recording automatically stops. This prevents premature stops if you briefly pass through your home area. Range: 1–10 min. Default: 3 min.

Backup

  • Export All Trips — Exports all trips, track points, groups, notes, and settings into a single .pathreel file. Use the iOS share sheet to save it to Files, AirDrop, or cloud storage. This is a full backup of your travel data.
  • Import Backup — Import a previously exported .pathreel file. Duplicate trips (matching start time and distance) are automatically skipped. Groups and notes are restored.

About

  • Version — Shows the current app version number.
  • Check for Updates — (iOS only) Checks the App Store for a newer version. If available, shows the version comparison and a direct link to update.

Tips

  • For best GPS accuracy, keep "Always Allow" location permission. "While Using" mode may stop recording when the app goes to the background.
  • Take photos during your trip — they automatically appear at the right location during replay.
  • Use satellite map mode for the most cinematic replays.
  • Export in 9:16 for vertical social media platforms, 16:9 for YouTube.
  • Search for attractions before visiting a new city — save them as check-in targets and let PathReel auto-stamp them as you travel.
  • Enable Auto Record if you commute daily — PathReel will track every trip without manual start/stop.
  • Use the "Read More" + "Listen" feature to learn about attractions hands-free while walking or driving.
  • All data is stored locally on your device. No accounts, no cloud, no tracking.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 6, 2026

Overview

PathReel is designed with privacy at its core. All your data stays on your device. We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal data to external servers.

Data Collection

PathReel does not collect: personal information, location data to any server, photos, or usage analytics.

On-Device Storage

All data is stored locally on your device: GPS track points, trip metadata, notes, and app settings.

Permissions

Location (Always) — Used solely to record GPS routes. Never leaves your device.
Photo Library — Used to display trip photos and save exported videos.

Third-Party Services

Apple Maps/MapKit — Map display and POI lookup via Apple's servers.
App Store — Update check via iTunes API. No personal data sent.
Attraction Data — UNESCO API, Wikivoyage API, Geoapify, US NPS API — used to search nearby attractions. Only coordinates are sent; no personal data.
AI Descriptions — Groq API (Llama 3.3) — generates attraction descriptions. Only the attraction name and category are sent.
Google Translate — Used to translate Wikipedia articles. Only article text is sent.
Nominatim (OpenStreetMap) — Used for address geocoding in Auto Record home location setup. Only the address text is sent.

Data Sharing

When you share via the iOS share sheet, PathReel provides the file to your chosen platform. PathReel itself does not upload anything.

Children's Privacy

PathReel does not knowingly collect information from children under 13.
